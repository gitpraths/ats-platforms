# Video 3 — Hiring Board
**Duration:** ~5 minutes
**Login as:** admin@myats.dev / password123

---

## Pre-recording Checklist
- [ ] App is running
- [ ] Navigate to http://localhost:5173/hiring-board before recording
- [ ] Confirm cards are visible (14 Australian applications should appear)

---

## Script

### [0:00–0:45] Board Overview
**ACTION:** Show the full Hiring Board with all Kanban columns visible.

**NARRATE:**
> "The Hiring Board is where the recruitment team manages every active candidate
> through the hiring pipeline. Each column represents a stage:
> Applied, Screening, Interview, Offer, Hired, and Rejected."

**ACTION:** Pan slowly across the columns left to right.

**NARRATE:**
> "Each card shows the candidate's name, which job they applied for, their score if rated,
> and how long ago they applied. The board shows all applications across all jobs
> so you have the full picture in one view."

---

### [0:45–1:30] Drag and Drop — Moving a Candidate
**ACTION:** Find a card in the Applied column, e.g. any candidate.

**NARRATE:**
> "The most powerful feature of the board is drag and drop.
> When a candidate progresses through the process, we simply drag their card
> to the next stage."

**ACTION:** Slowly drag a card from **Applied** to **Screening**. Pause after dropping.

**NARRATE:**
> "The stage is updated instantly in the database.
> No page refresh needed — the change is saved the moment you drop the card."

**ACTION:** Drag another card from **Screening** to **Interview**.

**NARRATE:**
> "Let's move this candidate forward to Interview stage.
> The pipeline funnel on the Dashboard will reflect this change next time it refreshes."

---

### [1:30–2:15] Moving to Offer and Hired
**ACTION:** Drag a card to **Offer**.

**NARRATE:**
> "When a candidate receives an offer, we move them to Offer stage.
> This flags them for the hiring manager to follow up."

**ACTION:** Drag the same card to **Hired**.

**NARRATE:**
> "Once confirmed — Hired. This automatically counts toward the 'Hired This Month'
> metric on the Dashboard. At this point the recruiter can create a Placement record
> which triggers the welfare check schedule."

---

### [2:15–3:00] Rejecting a Candidate
**ACTION:** Drag a card to **Rejected**.

**NARRATE:**
> "Rejected applications move to the Rejected column.
> They stay visible on the board for record-keeping but are excluded from
> the active application count in stats."

---

### [3:00–3:45] Filtering the Board
**ACTION:** Use the search/filter bar at the top of the board if available.

**NARRATE:**
> "With many open roles, the board can get busy.
> You can filter by job title or candidate name to focus on a specific role
> or find a particular applicant quickly."

**ACTION:** Type a job title like "Retail" in the filter.

**NARRATE:**
> "The board narrows to only show cards matching that job — making it easy
> for a recruiter to manage their specific roles without distraction."

**ACTION:** Clear the filter.

---

### [3:45–4:30] List View Toggle
**ACTION:** If there is a list/table view toggle, click it to switch to list view.

**NARRATE:**
> "Some recruiters prefer a list view over the Kanban layout —
> especially when managing a large number of applications.
> The list view shows the same data in a sortable table format."

**ACTION:** Switch back to Kanban view.

---

### [4:30–5:00] Stage Change Dialog
**ACTION:** Click on a card to open it, or click a stage-change button within a card.

**NARRATE:**
> "You can also change a candidate's stage by clicking into their card
> and selecting the new stage from a dropdown. This is useful on mobile
> or when you want to add a note at the same time."

**ACTION:** Change stage via the dialog, add a note, confirm.

**NARRATE:**
> "The note is saved with the stage change and visible in the candidate's history.
> In the next video we'll dive into the Candidates module —
> where you manage full candidate profiles."

**ACTION:** Stop recording.
