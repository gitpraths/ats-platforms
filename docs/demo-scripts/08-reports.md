## Script

### [0:00–0:30] Reports Overview
**ACTION:** Show the Reports page — three tabs visible at the top.

**NARRATE:**
> "The Reports module gives administrators and managers insight into
> how the recruitment operation is performing.
> There are three report views: Provider Performance, Placement Details,
> and Staff Activity. Let's walk through each one."

---

### [0:30–1:30] Tab 1 — Provider Performance
**ACTION:** Click the **Provider Performance** tab (should be default).

**NARRATE:**
> "The Provider Performance report shows how effective each employment service provider
> has been at placing candidates into work.
> For each provider we can see the total number of candidates they support,
> how many have been placed, and their placement rate as a percentage."

**ACTION:** Point to the placement rate column.

**NARRATE:**
> "Placement rate is the key metric here — it's candidates placed divided by
> total candidates supported, expressed as a percentage.
> A high placement rate means the provider is successfully moving candidates
> from job-seeker to employed."

**ACTION:** Show the date filter at the top. Change the date range to last 3 months.

**NARRATE:**
> "Date filters let us look at performance over a specific period —
> last month, last quarter, or a custom range.
> This is useful for monthly performance reviews or annual reporting."

**ACTION:** Click **Export CSV**.

**NARRATE:**
> "Every report can be exported to CSV with one click.
> The exported file includes all columns and all rows within the selected date range —
> ready to drop into Excel or send to a funding body."

---

### [1:30–2:30] Tab 2 — Placement Details
**ACTION:** Click the **Placement Details** tab.

**NARRATE:**
> "The Placement Details report gives a row-by-row view of every placement —
> candidate name, provider, employer, job title, start date, and confirmation status."

**ACTION:** Point to the welfare check dots column.

**NARRATE:**
> "The welfare check column shows the five milestone dots for each placement —
> the same visual indicator from the Placements page.
> This lets a manager scan down the report and immediately identify
> any placement where welfare follow-ups are overdue."

**ACTION:** Apply a date filter — e.g. placements created this month.

**NARRATE:**
> "Filtering by date shows only placements created within that window.
> This is useful for monthly outcome reporting — for example, showing
> all placements made in March 2026."

**ACTION:** Click **Export CSV**.

**NARRATE:**
> "Exported with welfare check status included per row —
> useful for DES compliance submissions."

---

### [2:30–3:30] Tab 3 — Staff Activity
**ACTION:** Click the **Staff Activity** tab.

**NARRATE:**
> "The Staff Activity report shows which recruiters are managing which jobs
> and how many applications each recruiter is handling.
> This helps team leaders balance workload across the team."

**ACTION:** Point to the job count and application count columns per recruiter.

**NARRATE:**
> "We can see Emma is managing three jobs with eleven applications assigned to her,
> while Liam is across two jobs. If the team takes on more work,
> a manager can use this report to redistribute assignments."

**ACTION:** Apply a date filter.

**NARRATE:**
> "Filtering by date shows recruiter activity within a period —
> useful for performance reviews."

**ACTION:** Click **Export CSV**.
