1.What the Training Module does

A single place to manage the courses your candidates take and the certificates they earn — past, current, and future.

Three views, one purpose

  • Catalogue — admins maintain the list of courses (name, code, provider, duration).
  • Per-candidate history — every candidate's profile shows the training they've done, are doing, or are scheduled to do.
  • Cross-candidate operations — recruiters get a global view of enrolments and a bulk-enrol flow for cohorts.

Status lifecycle

Every enrolment has one of five statuses. Move freely between them as situations change.

StatusMeans
enrolledBooked but hasn't started.
in progressCurrently undertaking the course.
completedFinished. Certificate number can be recorded.
withdrawnCandidate withdrew before completion.
failedDid not pass.
Behind the scenes: while a candidate has an in progress enrolment, the start/end dates show up as the "active training" pill on their profile automatically — no extra step needed.

2.The training catalogue (admins)

Maintained at /admin/trainings. Visible to recruiters as read-only; admins and recruiter-admins can add, edit, and deactivate.

Adding a course

1
Click the dark + New Training button in the top-right of the catalogue.
2
Fill in Name (required). Tip: keep names short and readable — they appear in dropdowns all over the platform.
3
Optionally set Code (e.g. CHC33015 for a TAFE course), Duration (days) (drives the auto-suggested end date on cohort enrol), Provider, and a Description.
4
Click Create. The course is immediately available everywhere.

Editing or deactivating

Click the pencil icon on any row. Use the Mark inactive link inside the edit dialog to hide a course from new enrolment flows — existing enrolment records keep working. Inactive courses don't appear in the catalogue picker on the Cohort enrol page.

Search and filter

The search box matches both the name and the code (case-insensitive). Toggle Active only off to see deactivated courses.

Why can't I delete a course? Once a course has been used in an enrolment, hard-deleting it would lose history. We deactivate instead — the course disappears from the picker but the history of who took it stays intact.

3.Enrolling one candidate

When you need to add a single training record to a single candidate — by far the most common task.

Where to find it

Open any candidate from Candidates. Scroll down past Application History and you'll find the Training history panel.

Adding an enrolment

1
Click the dark + Enrol button on the right of the panel header.
2
Course * — pick from the catalogue dropdown (only active courses appear).
3
Status — defaults to enrolled. Choose in progress if the candidate is already studying; the dates immediately flow to their profile's "active training" indicator.
4
Start date / End date — both optional, but enter end if known. The system rejects an end date that's earlier than start.
5
If Status = Completed, a Certificate # field appears. Fill it in to record the certificate number.
6
Click Enrol. The row appears immediately in the candidate's history table.

Editing or removing an enrolment

Click Edit on any row in the history table. The same dialog opens, pre-filled. Use it to update the status (e.g. move from enrolled to in progress, or to completed), correct a date, or add a certificate number. The Remove link at the bottom of the dialog deletes the enrolment entirely — useful for fixing mistakes.

Did you know? The status badge colour in the history table matches the same colours used elsewhere in the platform — grey for enrolled, blue for in progress, green for completed, amber for withdrawn, red for failed. Spot a candidate's situation at a glance.

4.The Training page — Enrolments tab

A cross-candidate operational view. Access via the Training entry in the main nav (between Candidates and Placements).

What you see first

Five summary chips at the top show how many enrolments are in each status — driven by your active filters (excluding the status filter itself). Below that, a filter bar; below that, a paginated table of every enrolment in the system.

The filter bar

ControlUse it for
Status chipsClick one or more to narrow by status. Tap again to deselect.
All coursesLimit to one course (handy when reviewing who took a specific qualification).
All providersLimit to one training provider.
From / ToDate range on the enrolment's start date.
Search candidate nameCase-insensitive partial match.

A Clear filters link appears when anything is set.

The table

Each row shows the candidate, the course, the provider, the status, dates, and a certificate number (if recorded). The Open link on the right takes you straight to the candidate's full profile — useful when reviewing a list of completions before sending out paperwork.

Pagination

The page shows 25 enrolments at a time. Use the Prev / Next buttons at the bottom of the table. The total count is always visible.

Empty list? Either your filter is too tight (click Clear filters) or there genuinely are no enrolments yet. New deployments start with an empty list — start by creating courses in the catalogue and enrolling at least one candidate.

5.Cohort enrolment — bulk enrol

When a group of candidates is doing the same course at the same time, enrolling them one by one is busywork. The Cohort enrol tab does the whole group in one transaction.

Three steps, one page

On the Training page, click the Cohort enrol tab. The form is a single inline flow — each step unlocks the next.

1
Pick a course. The selected course card shows the name, code, provider and the nominal duration in days.
2
Set dates. Enter a start date. If the course has a duration set, the end date auto-fills (e.g. 180-day Cert III sets end date 180 days later). Both dates are editable.
3
Choose candidates. Search by name, tick the checkbox next to each candidate. Your running selection is in the sticky right-hand panel — remove anyone with the × icon.

Submitting

The sticky footer at the bottom of the page shows how many candidates are selected. When at least one is picked, the Enrol N candidates button activates. Click it. A short pause, then a results dialog:

  • Created N enrolments — how many succeeded.
  • Skipped (collapsible) — any candidates who already had an active enrolment in this course are listed with the reason. They're left alone; their existing record isn't disturbed.

Skip logic

A candidate is skipped when they already hold an enrolled or in progress record for the same course. This prevents accidental double-booking. Once their previous enrolment moves to completed, withdrawn, or failed, you can re-enrol them in a future cohort.

"View enrolments" handoff

After a successful bulk enrol, click View enrolments in the results dialog. You're switched to the Enrolments tab with the course and start date pre-filtered — you can immediately see the new cohort in context.

All-or-nothing transaction: if one insert fails (e.g. an invalid candidate ID), the entire batch is rolled back. You'll get an error message instead of partial creation. Try a smaller batch to isolate the cause.
